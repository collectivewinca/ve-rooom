# Key Discussion Points

## 1. Pitch Preparation & Presentation Strategy

The team discussed that successful pitching is not only about having the correct solution but also about:

* Understanding how investors perceive the presenters.
* Rehearsing delivery, body language, confidence, and reactions.
* Being fully prepared with evidence rather than assumptions.
* Remaining authentic instead of attempting to impress through theatrics.

It was emphasized that:

* Confidence comes from preparation.
* Team members should anticipate difficult questions.
* Investor meetings require rehearsed responses and polished communication.

---

## 2. Human Psychology During Pitches

A significant portion of the discussion centered around interpersonal dynamics during meetings.

Key observations included:

* People often judge speakers before evaluating ideas.
* Personality and presentation style influence investor perception.
* Some investors intentionally pressure founders during meetings.
* Maintaining composure under pressure is an essential leadership skill.

The team agreed that understanding these behavioral patterns will improve future presentations.

---

## 3. Beachhead Market Validation

The team reviewed progress on defining the startup's initial target market.

Current assessment:

* Customer conversations continue to validate the direction.
* Additional interviews are still required before finalizing assumptions.

Current concern:

* Finding new participants for customer discovery interviews.

## 5. Leadership & Company Culture

The team reflected extensively on leadership philosophy.

Major themes included:

* Leading through integrity rather than intimidation.
* Building trust across the team.
* Maintaining strong personal values despite corporate pressures.
* Supporting one another throughout the startup journey.

There was unanimous agreement that strong leadership and shared values are competitive advantages.

---

## 6. Understanding Corporate Decision-Making

Discussion explored:

* How corporate organizations operate using structured systems.
* Recognizing decision patterns used by accelerators and enterprise partners.
* Learning to communicate using language that aligns with stakeholder expectations without compromising authenticity.

The team believes understanding these systems will improve fundraising and partnership outcomes.

---

## 7. Accelerator / JSSA Preparation

The group discussed the upcoming accelerator interactions.

Key conclusions:

* Avoid trying to overly impress evaluators.
* Demonstrate intelligence, teamwork, and authenticity.
* Continue refining messaging before future presentations.
* Verify timelines regarding promotional announcements and LinkedIn posts.

---

## 8. Branding & Company Name

A lengthy discussion focused on branding.

Current company name:

**Amply**

Discussion points:

* The name has grown on several team members.
* It may still be revisited before public promotion.
* Future branding should:

  * Be memorable
  * Be short
  * Be easy to pronounce
  * Function naturally as a verb (similar to Uber or DoorDash)

The team agreed to generate alternative names before making a final decision.
