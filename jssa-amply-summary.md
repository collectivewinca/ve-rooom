## Meeting Summary

This was a strategic planning and team alignment meeting for the Amply startup team. The 106-minute conversation covered preparations for an upcoming pitch to JSSA (an investor/accelerator program), company branding and naming strategy, corporate culture and presentation psychology, and customer discovery efforts. The tone was candid, energetic, and deeply reflective — blending tactical pitch preparation with philosophical discussions about leadership, values, and the nature of corporate environments. Key participants included Don, Ruckmo (CTO/lead), and at least one other team member, with Aaron mentioned as an external coordinator.

## Key Topics Discussed

- **JSSA Pitch Strategy**: The team discussed JSSA's structured program — each participant has specific goals, and the team needs to confirm what JSSA wants to hear. The consensus was to be authentic ("impress them to show who we are") rather than trying to game the system, since JSSA is "walking them through the process" and wants to help them succeed.

- **Corporate Presentation Psychology**: An extended discussion about how perception affects pitching — people judge based on appearance and demeanor, not just content. The team shared stories about "smiling your way through" and the importance of rehearsing not just the pitch but "reaction to how we look, how we sound." One member shared a personal story about spending two years deliberately working on being perceived as "nice" rather than "aggressive and intimidating."

- **Company Branding and Name Change**: The team debated whether to rebrand from "Amply" after August 7th. Ideas included finding 50 name options then narrowing to 5, with criteria that the name be one word, easy to remember, app-friendly, and work as a verb (like "Uber it" or "DoorDash it"). The name should also connect to the business mission. There was debate about whether "Amply" has grown on the team and if it's worth keeping.

- **Customer Discovery**: One member conducted 27 customer conversations, capturing demographics and insights in a 7-page document using ChatGPT to summarize. The data includes conversations with people at a funeral, mostly without formal education. A BMW-driving couple in their 70s still needs to be contacted.

- **Secure Infrastructure**: Discussion of the technical platform — using Cloudflare for video streaming, building a secure self-hosted alternative to Zoom with AI integration for meeting summaries and action items. Emphasis on keeping proprietary data ("alpha") away from general-purpose AI models like ChatGPT. The team has deployed their own AI model to eliminate cloud dependencies.

- **Leadership and Values**: Deep discussion about Ruckmo's leadership style — described as principled, values-driven, and resilient ("she's worked at Massacad for almost 24 years"). The team contrasted this with typical corporate leadership that "takes principles from war books and twists them" to benefit the company at the expense of people.
